Trends, impulses, correction

Today we will raise such topics as trends, impulses and corrections.
 Let's start with the definition of the trend. Without an understanding, what is the trend now, do not even start trading in the financial markets. We need to understand what kind of trends are that they represent

Trend is the general direction of price movement, which is confirmed by major players.

All trends are divided into three types: ascending, downward and flat.

Trends in the direction - it is ascending or downward, they are also called bullish and bearish, respectively.

The third trend type is the lack of trend, in other words, Flat.

Flat on the stock exchange is such a state of the financial market in which the price varies within the range with well-distinguisible borders. At the same time, quotes are often suitable directly to the borders, confirming their significance for traders, but they cannot exit abroad. This type of trend is just a lateral movement in which, as a rule, a set of positions are a set of positions.

As soon as we see the momentum up, we have two points, minimum and maximum. If after the correction, we have a new impulse, with an updated vertex of a pre-running pulse, if we connect two points from below and on top, we will see the formation of a new ascending trend.

Trends are directed, temporary and trend in motion

Trend in time is the time timeframe where you are seeing the price of the asset, the older timeframe, the stronger the trend. Always need to be focused on the movement of the main trend on day or weekly chart.

Trend in motion - this type of trend is divided into exponential, normal and gentle (weak trend).

In the market, the main thing is to remember one rule that the more vertical trend, the more likely the degree of probability, it will also fly down / up. Vertical trend, very often occurs on altcoins. A vivid example of a vertical trend can be observed on the chart of any altcoins and the Bitcoin itself.
